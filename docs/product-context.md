# Orbit Product Context

## Vision

Build an assistant that understands enough of a person's life to coordinate their digital world, while requiring the fewest possible interruptions and always keeping the user in control.

Orbit is not another chatbot and not another workflow builder. It is an approachable orchestration experience that hides technical complexity and helps non-expert users receive practical value from AI.

## Target user

The initial user is someone who:

- uses email, calendar, contacts, tasks, and a smartphone
- may own smart-home or wearable devices
- is curious about AI but does not want to configure agents or workflows
- feels overwhelmed by fragmented apps, forgotten commitments, and information overload
- values convenience but is sensitive to privacy and accidental actions

## Everyday problems

- Important email is buried.
- Calendar conflicts are noticed too late.
- People forget follow-ups, renewals, appointments, bills, medication, maintenance, and family events.
- Information is scattered across email, notes, files, messages, and apps.
- Smart-home routines require multiple applications.
- Health context is disconnected from schedule and environment.
- Existing AI tools require users to know what to ask.
- Existing automation platforms require technical setup.

## Core experience

Orbit quietly gathers authorized context and surfaces a small number of useful observations.

Examples:

- “Your dentist appointment is tomorrow, and the insurance card attached to last month’s email is newer than the one in your files.”
- “You slept poorly, your first meeting moved earlier, and your planned workout is intense. Would you like a lighter plan drafted?”
- “Your flight lands after the scheduled meeting. I found two available alternatives and drafted a reschedule message.”
- “Movie night is starting. I can set the receiver, lights, and fan using the existing home platform.”

## Differentiation

Orbit does not attempt to win by owning every connector or building a proprietary model.

Orbit differentiates through:

- consumer-friendly onboarding
- normalized personal and household context
- attention and prioritization
- permission-aware actions
- explainable recommendations
- cross-domain reasoning
- voice interaction design
- audit and undo
- provider independence

## Initial non-goals

- Building custom email, calendar, speech, smart-home, or workflow infrastructure
- Supporting every provider at launch
- Autonomous purchasing
- Medical diagnosis
- Fully autonomous communication
- Replacing Home Assistant
- Replacing ChatGPT
- Building dedicated hardware
- Building a generalized multi-agent framework
- Commercial-scale multi-tenancy during the personal prototype phase

## Trust model

Orbit should be useful before it can act.

Default progression:

- Connect
- Observe
- Explain
- Recommend
- Draft
- Request approval
- Execute
- Verify
- Undo

Permissions should be capability-specific and understandable to nontechnical users.
