# Orbit Visual Refinement Comparison

## Previous implementation diagnosis

Fresh evidence was captured in `design/audit/refinement-before/`.

- The attention headline occupied most of the reading surface.
- Maya and Presence formed a left-side profile rail disconnected from the concern.
- A long elliptical line communicated decoration rather than product state.
- The persistent utility navigation competed with the concern.
- Presence disappeared from conversation and proposal review.
- The Lab placed controls before personality and used small previews that resembled loaders.

## Structural directions evaluated

### Presence-centered

Best fit. Orbit leads the vertical relationship, Maya remains present through greeting and context, and the focal concern stays editorial without becoming a marketing hero.

### Relationship-centered

Rejected for this pass. A paired user marker and Presence risk recreating the profile-card problem and require more persistent geometry than the product needs.

### Attention-centered

Useful for Focus screens, but insufficient as the primary identity model because Presence can become secondary to the concern.

## Before and after

| Surface | Before | Refined |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Main composition | Left profile rail plus oversized concern | Presence-centered vertical relationship |
| User representation | Permanent initials avatar | Quiet greeting and contextual personalization |
| Orbit identity | Small mark beside avatar | Large primary identity, retained through conversation and approval |
| Paths | Decorative elliptical line | Open paths appear only for semantic Presence states |
| Navigation | Four persistent utility links | Compact accessible menu |
| Evidence | Always-visible fact row | Progressive `Why this matters` disclosure |
| Lab priority | Controls first | Large live personality preview first |
| Comparison | Five small samples | Five equal large previews in the same state |
| Context | Three small cards | Three full-size realistic frames with state-specific behavior |

## Live variant evaluation

| Variant | Strength | Remaining risk |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Mark | Most recognizable and scalable | Less expressive while speaking |
| Pulse | Warmest listening behavior | Needs the satellite relationship to feel uniquely Orbit |
| Trail | Clearest speaking identity | Can feel mechanical if used outside speaking or thinking |
| Constellation | Strong relationship metaphor | Highest distraction and weakest small-size recognition |
| Hybrid | Best balance of warmth, state clarity, and brand identity | Must keep path and glow restrained |

Hybrid is the provisional recommendation after live evaluation. It remains locally selectable and is not a permanent product decision.
