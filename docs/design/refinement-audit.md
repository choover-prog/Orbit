# Orbit Refinement Audit

## Evidence

1. `design/audit/refinement-before/01-quiet-orbit-attention.png` — main attention state.
2. `design/audit/refinement-before/02-presence-lab.png` — original Presence Lab.

## Findings

1. **Main attention state — needs refinement.** The concern is understandable, but headline scale and the left profile rail divide attention. The decorative path has no semantic role, and Orbit is visually too small.
2. **Presence Lab — needs refinement.** All controls arrive before the live identity. The three small context cards and five small comparison samples test settings more strongly than personality, warmth, or state clarity.

## Highest-impact corrections

- Center Orbit Presence and remove the persistent user rail.
- Reduce focal type and keep one supporting sentence.
- Hide source facts behind progressive disclosure.
- Keep Presence adjacent to conversation and approval content.
- Lead the Lab with a large live preview and move controls below it.
- Give listening, thinking, and speaking distinct static and moving treatments.

## Accessibility evidence limits

Screenshots establish hierarchy, density, and visible state treatment only. Keyboard behavior, status labels, focus, motion preferences, reduced motion, contrast, and responsive overflow require live browser and automated checks.
