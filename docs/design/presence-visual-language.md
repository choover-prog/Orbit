# Orbit Presence Visual Language

Orbit Presence is the user-facing identity of Orbit. It is not a spinner, status badge, microphone replacement, or decorative logo. It stays close to the active content and communicates availability, attention, listening, thought, speech, completion, and calm.

## Shared anatomy

Every variant uses a restrained subset of the same visual vocabulary:

- one directional spark or a small number of particles for relationship and context;
- an open path only when a relationship or active state needs to be expressed;
- a soft inner field for receptivity and warmth;
- Orbit blue for orientation, speech, or attention rather than constant decoration.

Closed mechanical rings are avoided. Paths are asymmetric and incomplete so the mark reads as a living relationship rather than an indeterminate loader. No variant uses a dominant dark center inside a surrounding field: this prevents Orbit from reading as an iris or pupil. Geometry remains abstract: no planet, globe, literal satellite, waveform, rainbow, or science-fiction interface.

## Variant language

### Mark

An open circular gesture, two restrained internal ticks, and one satellite. It remains recognizable at small sizes and behaves like a living brand signature without a pupil-like center.

### Pulse

An asymmetric soft field, diagonal glint, and offset spark. Filled translucent shapes replace concentric loader rings, and the missing central orb prevents an eye-like read. Listening is its clearest state.

### Trail

One satellite and a pair of conversational open paths. The luminous tail appears most clearly while speaking and is intentionally absent or faint while idle.

### Constellation

Five low-count particles connected by curved relationships, with no single dominant center. Links strengthen during thinking; particles gather or flow only when the state calls for it.

### Hybrid

Combines Pulse warmth, Trail identity, a single satellite, listening breath, thinking path, and speaking tail without enclosing a dark core. It remains a provisional production candidate, not a permanent selection.

### Ribbon

An independent direction outside the orbital-ring vocabulary. Two calligraphic paths move through a loose S-curve between a quiet origin and a bright destination. It feels gestural and conversational, cannot be mistaken for an eye, and is intentionally exploratory rather than a new default.

## Scale and proximity

- Small: compact navigation or secondary status use.
- Medium: conversation and approval proximity.
- Large: the main Quiet Orbit relationship and Presence Lab comparisons.
- Studio hero: an intentionally larger preview for evaluating personality.

Presence should remain visually adjacent to the content Orbit is expressing. It must not be isolated in a profile rail or reduced to a corner badge during an active conversation.
